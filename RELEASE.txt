Nadiloka -- Release Notes
=========================

Version 0.2.0 (2026-07-02)
---------------------------
- Phase v0.2 (Space and the Tejas field): light patches ignite, live,
  fade, and reappear across the grid. NADI-005 through NADI-008.
- Grid: the bounded W x H spatial scaffold with (x, y) coordinates, no
  edge wrapping, explicit out-of-bounds errors, and deterministic
  row-major neighbourhood queries clipped at the edges (NADI-005).
- Tejas field: per-cell light energy in 0..FOOD_MAX with clamped
  illumination over patch discs, plus the take() read/decrement seam
  v1.2's harvest will feed through; Patch{center, radius, intensity,
  lifetime} model (NADI-006).
- Patch lifecycle in the update-Tejas phase: age, linear fade, expiry,
  and seeded respawn holding the target live-patch count; the initial
  batch ignites staggered so total energy holds a steady level
  (NADI-007).
- Counters and the guard: the counter line now carries total field
  energy and live patches; the energy-band test (150..1500 over 300
  ticks, no cell above FOOD_MAX) pins the no-runaway-no-collapse DoD
  (NADI-008).

Version 0.1.0 (2026-07-02)
---------------------------
- Phase v0.1 (Skeleton and the Meru tick): an empty world ticks
  deterministically. NADI-001 through NADI-004.
- Project skeleton: the nadiloka/ engine package, demos/, tests/;
  ruff + pytest tooling; dependencies exactly pysm + pyyaml (NADI-001).
- World container: WorldConfig with an explicit seed, one World-owned
  seeded random.Random (module-level random.* forbidden and pinned by
  test), a tick counter, and the empty population dict (NADI-002).
- Meru tick: World.step() calls the five phase hooks in fixed order
  (update Tejas, rebuild grid, act, flush Nadi, death/cleanup); the
  order is pinned by a recording-spy test (NADI-003).
- Observability: demos/empty.py runner (--ticks, required --seed) and
  the read-only counters scaffold (tick, population, energy); the
  determinism harness asserts byte-identical counter logs for two
  same-seed runs, in-process and through the CLI (NADI-004).
