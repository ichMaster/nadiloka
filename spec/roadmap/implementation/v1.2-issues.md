## v1.2 — Feeding and metabolism

### NADI-020 — Harvest from the Tejas field

**Description:**
The first energy flow in the world: the Bindu body-tick harvests light. Harvest takes up to `harvest.gain` from the digitant's cell and its `harvest.radius` neighbourhood, **capped by what the field actually holds**, and debits the field by exactly what the agent gains — energy is conserved, never created (phase 3 feeding on phase 1's field, ARCHITECTURE §One tick cycle). (`nadiloka/digitant.py` body-tick + `nadiloka/tejas.py` debit.)

**What needs to be done:**
- A harvest step in the body-tick reading the Jati `harvest` block (`gain`, `radius`): collect energy from the digitant's cell and the cells within `harvest.radius` (the v0.2 neighbourhood helpers), up to `gain` per tick.
- Cap by field content: a cell yields at most what it holds; total harvested never exceeds `gain`; no cell goes below zero.
- Debit the field by every unit the agent gains — strict conservation, no duplication, no leakage.
- Credit the digitant's energy need, clamped to its range from the Jati `needs` block.
- A deterministic draw order over the neighbourhood cells (stable coordinate ordering — never dict/hash order), so same-seed runs harvest identically.
- Counter: harvested-this-tick (joined into the v1 counter set by NADI-022).

**Dependencies:** NADI-019, NADI-007 (v0.2 — the Tejas field lifecycle)

**Expected result:**
Bindu on lit cells drain measurable energy from the field into themselves; the field is visibly drawn down where they feed; not one unit of energy is created or lost in the exchange.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] Harvest collects up to `harvest.gain` from the cell + `harvest.radius` neighbourhood, capped by field content
- [ ] Unit test: energy conservation on harvest — field loss equals agent gain, clamped
- [ ] No cell goes negative; agent energy stays clamped to its needs range
- [ ] The Tejas field is visibly drawn down where Bindu feed (field-energy counter + renderer)
- [ ] Determinism: same seed produces an identical harvest sequence and counter log

### NADI-021 — Metabolism, needs decay, and mode re-evaluation

**Description:**
The other half of the loop: per-tick metabolism and the brain reacting to it. Energy decays and hunger rises at the Jati `needs` rates; after the body acts, the brain re-evaluates its mode from the **fresh** needs — Idle/Hungry as **internal transitions**, so re-evaluation never re-fires `on_enter` (ARCHITECTURE §Two-clock, §Key invariants and pitfalls). The body-tick stays pure arithmetic — the standing cheap-body invariant. (`nadiloka/digitant.py`.)

**What needs to be done:**
- Body-tick metabolism: energy decays per tick and hunger rises, both at rates from the Jati `needs` block; values clamped to their ranges.
- Order within the body-tick: harvest (NADI-020) then metabolism, so a fed Bindu's net energy reflects both sides of the loop each tick.
- Add the Hungry mode next to Idle on the NADI-017 pysm base; thresholds from the Jati; the brain reads the post-body needs, never stale values.
- Idle/Hungry re-evaluation as internal transitions wherever the mode does not change — no `on_enter` re-fire (extending NADI-017's pinning tests to the two-mode machine).
- Preserve the two-clock order: the body acts in the current mode, then the brain picks the next one.
- Keep the whole loop pure arithmetic — no allocation-heavy or I/O work in the hot body-tick.

**Dependencies:** NADI-017, NADI-020

**Expected result:**
Bindu on lit cells net-gain energy and sit in Idle; Bindu in darkness net-lose, cross the hunger threshold, and switch to Hungry — deterministically, with no spurious `on_enter` re-fires.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] Energy decays and hunger rises per tick at the Jati rates; values stay clamped
- [ ] Unit test: Bindu on a lit fixture field net-gain energy; Bindu in darkness net-lose
- [ ] The brain re-evaluates Idle/Hungry from fresh needs after the body acts (two-clock order pinned)
- [ ] Unit test: mode re-evaluation is an internal transition — no `on_enter` re-fire
- [ ] Determinism: same seed yields identical needs and mode traces over M ticks

### NADI-022 — Health model + species counters

**Description:**
Health as the slow consequence of the needs: it **falls under sustained starvation, recovers slowly when fed**, and zero is the death-threshold state — the trigger v1.3's cleanup phase (NADI-024) removes on. Plus the v1 species counters that make the phase measurable (ARCHITECTURE §Counters and measurement). (`nadiloka/digitant.py` + counters.)

**What needs to be done:**
- Health dynamics in the body-tick: under sustained starvation (hunger past its threshold / energy exhausted) health falls at the Jati `life` rate; when fed, it recovers slowly; clamped to its range.
- Zero health marks the death-threshold state on the digitant; actual removal is v1.3's cleanup phase (NADI-024) — nothing is deleted outside phase 5.
- Counters: mean energy and mean health per species, plus harvested-this-tick (from NADI-020), joined into the counter log and the renderer header.
- Counters emitted as the structured log lines the determinism harness compares byte-for-byte (ARCHITECTURE §Testing and CI).
- All health rates and thresholds read from `bindu.yaml` (`life`, `needs`) — nothing tunable in engine code (ARCHITECTURE §Configuration and parameters).

**Dependencies:** NADI-021

**Expected result:**
The v1.2 DoD holds end to end: Bindu on lit cells accumulate energy and stay healthy; Bindu in darkness starve and lose health; the field is drawn down where they feed — all visible in the counters.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] Unit test: sustained starvation lowers health; feeding recovers it (both directions)
- [ ] Zero health sets the death-threshold state; no removal happens outside the cleanup phase
- [ ] Counters: mean energy, mean health, harvested-this-tick in the structured log and the renderer header
- [ ] All health rates/thresholds come from the Jati `life`/`needs` blocks, not engine constants
- [ ] Determinism: two same-seed runs produce identical counter logs
