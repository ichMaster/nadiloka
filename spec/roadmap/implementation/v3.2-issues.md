# v3.2 — GitHub Issues

Issues for phase **v3.2 — Producer signals** (version **v3 — Nadi and communication**), derived from the per-phase Tasks list in [ROADMAP.md](../../ROADMAP.md) (§v3.2). This file is scoped to a single phase; v3.1 has its own breakdown. Each issue ships with the tests that encode its DoD (see [ARCHITECTURE.md](../../ARCHITECTURE.md) §Testing and CI).

v3.2 is the **first collective behaviour**: Bindu talk. They emit `crowd_signal` under high local density and `bloom` on a rich Tejas zone — the first live rows of the topic protocol ([ARCHITECTURE.md](../../ARCHITECTURE.md) §Topic protocol) — and listening Bindu bias movement and reproduction: avoid overcrowding, settle toward blooms. A global Nadi on/off toggle plus clustering/evenness counters make the effect measurable, and the closing smoke run encodes the v3 readiness criterion: same seed, Nadi on vs off, measurably different settlement, both runs stable. Conditions and thresholds live in the Jati, never in engine code.

## v3.2 — Producer signals

### NADI-033 — `crowd_signal` and `bloom` emission

**Description:**
Bindu emit the first producer signals over the v3.1 bus: `crowd_signal` when local density is high, `bloom` on a rich Tejas zone (the v3.2 rows of the topic protocol, [ARCHITECTURE.md](../../ARCHITECTURE.md) §Topic protocol). Emission happens in the body-tick via `emit()` — queued during the act phase, delivered at that tick's flush — and every condition comes from the Jati, not from engine constants. (`nadiloka/digitant.py`, `species/bindu.yaml`.)

**What needs to be done:**
- `crowd_signal`: during the body-tick, a Bindu whose local neighbour density (via the spatial hash) exceeds its Jati threshold emits `crowd_signal` with radius addressing.
- `bloom`: a Bindu sitting on a rich Tejas zone (local field energy above its Jati threshold) emits `bloom` with radius addressing.
- Declare both topics under `signals` in `species/bindu.yaml` (emits *and* listens), with thresholds and radii as descriptor parameters — nothing tunable in engine code (ARCHITECTURE §Configuration and parameters).
- Payloads carry what listeners need to react (sender position; density or zone richness), delivered into the recipient brain via `cargo` (NADI-032 wiring, untouched).
- Emissions ride the deferred-queue semantics of v3.1 unchanged: queued in the act phase, delivered at flush, replies next tick.
- Unit tests per ROADMAP §v3.2 Tests: `crowd_signal` fires exactly when density exceeds the threshold and stays silent below it; `bloom` fires exactly on a rich zone; emission counts are deterministic under a fixed seed.

**Dependencies:** NADI-032

**Expected result:**
Crowded Bindu broadcast `crowd_signal` and Bindu on rich light broadcast `bloom`, under exactly the Jati-declared conditions, through the standard deferred bus.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] `crowd_signal` emitted when local density (spatial hash) exceeds the Jati threshold — never below it
- [ ] `bloom` emitted on a rich Tejas zone per the Jati threshold — never elsewhere
- [ ] Thresholds, radii, and topic lists live in `species/bindu.yaml`, not in engine code
- [ ] Emissions queue during the act phase and deliver at that tick's flush (v3.1 semantics intact)
- [ ] Unit test: both emission conditions pinned, positive and negative cases; deterministic under seed

### NADI-034 — Listener reactions: settlement bias

**Description:**
The receiving side: Bindu getting `crowd_signal` bias movement and reproduction **away** from overcrowding; Bindu getting `bloom` settle **toward** rich zones. Reactions arrive as pysm events with the payload in `cargo` (NADI-032) and act as a bias on the existing v2 movement step — not a new movement engine. (`nadiloka/digitant.py`, `species/bindu.yaml`.)

**What needs to be done:**
- On `crowd_signal` delivery, bias the next movement steps away from the sender's position and suppress reproduction into the crowded neighbourhood (a raised effective energy bar or a skip near the crowd).
- On `bloom` delivery, bias the next movement steps toward the bloom position — settlement toward rich zones.
- Integrate as a weighting on the v2 step choice (NADI-026 wander / seek_light): candidate steps are re-scored by the active biases; ties still broken by the seeded world RNG (ARCHITECTURE §Key invariants — RNG discipline).
- Reactions on the brain are **internal transitions** (no `on_enter` re-fire — the pysm pitfall); bias strength and decay (how many ticks a received signal keeps influencing) declared in the Jati.
- Keep the body-tick cheap: reactions are arithmetic on stored bias state, no scans beyond the existing neighbourhood lookups.
- Unit tests per ROADMAP §v3.2 Tests: a Bindu receiving `crowd_signal` steps away from the sender and defers reproduction into the crowd; a Bindu receiving `bloom` steps toward it; reactions are deterministic under a fixed seed; no `on_enter` re-fires.

**Dependencies:** NADI-033, NADI-026

**Expected result:**
Received signals visibly steer settlement: crowds disperse, blooms attract — the first collective behaviour, expressed entirely through the bus and the Jati.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] Unit test: `crowd_signal` reception biases movement away from the sender and suppresses reproduction toward the crowd
- [ ] Unit test: `bloom` reception biases movement toward the bloom position
- [ ] Reactions are a bias on the v2 step choice; ties broken by the seeded world RNG (deterministic under seed)
- [ ] Reactions are internal transitions — a pinning test shows no `on_enter` re-fire
- [ ] Bias strength/decay come from `species/bindu.yaml`, not engine constants

### NADI-035 — Nadi toggle + clustering counters + smoke

**Description:**
The proof of the version: a **global Nadi on/off toggle**, clustering/evenness counters plus per-topic signal counts, and the smoke run that encodes the v3 readiness criterion — the same seed with Nadi on vs off yields **measurably different clustering counters, both runs stable** ([ROADMAP.md](../../ROADMAP.md) §v3.2 DoD). (`nadiloka/world.py`, `demos/bindu.py`, `tests/`.)

**What needs to be done:**
- A global Nadi on/off toggle in the world config (never a code constant, ARCHITECTURE §Configuration and parameters); with Nadi off, nothing is delivered and behaviour reverts to pure v2 — a stable baseline run.
- Clustering/evenness counters (e.g. neighbour-count distribution / occupancy dispersion) added to the counter set, alongside the per-topic emitted/delivered counts from NADI-032, emitted as structured log lines / CSV (ARCHITECTURE §Counters and measurement).
- Extend the Bindu demo (NADI-025) with a `--nadi on|off` switch; the renderer header shows the new counters live.
- Smoke test per ROADMAP §v3.2 Tests: two fixed-seed runs of K ticks, Nadi on vs off — clustering counters differ measurably (a more even spread, less overcrowding with Nadi on), and **both** runs keep the population in a healthy band (no explosion, no extinction).
- Determinism harness: with the toggle fixed either way, two same-seed runs produce byte-identical counter logs (ARCHITECTURE §Testing and CI) — the toggle itself is the only variable.

**Dependencies:** NADI-034, NADI-025

**Expected result:**
Toggling Nadi noticeably changes the settlement pattern, and the counters prove it — the Layer 4 readiness criterion, asserted by a smoke test.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] Global Nadi toggle lives in the world config; with Nadi off, behaviour reverts to the v2 baseline
- [ ] Clustering/evenness counters and per-topic signal counts emitted as structured logs; shown in the demo header
- [ ] `demos/bindu.py` gains a `--nadi on|off` switch
- [ ] Smoke test: same seed, Nadi on vs off → measurably different clustering counters; both runs stay in a healthy population band
- [ ] Determinism harness: fixed toggle + same seed → byte-identical counter logs across two runs
