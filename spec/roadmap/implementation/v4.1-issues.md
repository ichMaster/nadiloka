# v4.1 — GitHub Issues

Issues for phase **v4.1 — Chara and eating Bindu** (version **v4 — Consumers (Chara)**), derived from the per-phase Tasks list in [ROADMAP.md](../../ROADMAP.md) (§v4.1). This file is scoped to a single phase; v4.2 and v4.3 get their own breakdowns. Each issue ships with the tests that encode its DoD (see [ARCHITECTURE.md](../../ARCHITECTURE.md) §Testing and CI).

v4.1 opens the **second trophic level**: one digitant eats another and energy flows up. The eat action is wired **generically through Jati** — `ahara` names `tejas` or a prey species — so adding a level never touches the engine (ROADMAP §Notes on the ladder: "if a new species needs engine changes, the abstraction is leaking"). Predation follows the single rule set of ARCHITECTURE §Predation within a tick: the `eaten` flag, the body-tick liveness check, first-eater-wins, no double-eat. `species/chara.yaml` (`ahara: Bindu`) and the energy-per-level counter close the phase: roughly 10% of level-1 energy arrives at level 2 via `yields`/`gain` (ARCHITECTURE §Species descriptive language).

## v4.1 — Chara and eating Bindu

### NADI-036 — Generic `ahara` prey resolution

**Description:**
The targeting layer of the eat action, wired generically through Jati: `ahara` names either `tejas` (the v1 producer path) or a prey species (consumers, new here). Prey lookup goes through the spatial hash. Adding a trophic level must require **no engine change** — the declarative-growth rule (ROADMAP §Notes on the ladder; ARCHITECTURE §Species descriptive language). (`nadiloka/jati.py`, the body-tick feeding path.)

**What needs to be done:**
- Extend Jati validation (NADI-018): `ahara` accepts `tejas` or a known species name; unknown values fail loading with a clear error.
- A generic prey-resolution step in the body-tick feeding path: `ahara: tejas` keeps the v1.2 harvest path unchanged; a species-valued `ahara` resolves live prey of that species within `harvest.radius` via the spatial hash (NADI-019).
- Resolution filters out dead/eaten candidates (liveness against the current tick's flags — the groundwork NADI-037 builds on).
- Deterministic candidate order: prey candidates come back **id-sorted**, so the downstream choice never depends on hash/dict ordering (ARCHITECTURE §Key invariants and pitfalls).
- No species-name conditionals in the engine: the feeding path branches only on the *shape* of `ahara` (`tejas` vs species), never on `"Bindu"`/`"Chara"` literals.
- Keep the body-tick cheap: resolution is one spatial-hash query plus a filter — never a whole-population scan.

**Dependencies:** NADI-018, NADI-019

**Expected result:**
`ahara` is fully descriptor-driven: pointing it at `tejas` reproduces v1 harvesting byte-for-byte; pointing it at a species yields that species' in-range live individuals — with zero engine edits per new level.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] `ahara` validates as `tejas` or a known species; unknown names fail loading with a clear error
- [ ] `ahara: tejas` leaves the v1.2 harvest path unchanged (same-seed counter logs identical to pre-change)
- [ ] A species-valued `ahara` resolves in-range live prey via the spatial hash, id-sorted
- [ ] Unit test: resolution is descriptor-driven — a test-only species YAML with a different `ahara` resolves its prey with no engine change
- [ ] Unit test: dead/eaten candidates are filtered out of resolution

### NADI-037 — The eat action: `yields`→`gain`, eaten flag, no double-eat

**Description:**
The full predation rule set from ARCHITECTURE §Predation within a tick: co-located/in-range prey transfers its `yields` into the predator's `gain`; eating flags, the death phase removes; every agent checks liveness at body-tick start; the first eater in snapshot order wins; no double-eat within a tick. (The act phase + the death phase.)

**What needs to be done:**
- The eat action on the body-tick: when prey resolved by NADI-036 is co-located or within `harvest.radius`, transfer the prey's `yields` to the eater's energy through the eater's `gain` — the ~10% pyramid ratio lives entirely in these two Jati numbers (ARCHITECTURE §Species descriptive language).
- Eating sets the `eaten` flag on the prey; removal happens in phase 5 (death and cleanup), never mid-act.
- Liveness check at the start of every body-tick: an agent flagged `eaten` (or at zero health) earlier in the same act phase does not act this tick.
- First-eater-wins: a flagged prey is invisible to later eaters in snapshot order; the second would-be eater misses and stays hungry — no double transfer.
- Extend the death phase (NADI-024): `eaten` agents are removed alongside zero-health/lifespan removals and counted in the deaths counter.
- Energy accounting: what the prey yields is exactly what enters the transfer — no energy invented or lost outside the deliberate `yields`/`gain` pyramid loss.

**Dependencies:** NADI-036

**Expected result:**
Predation runs inside the deterministic act phase: one eat per prey per tick, energy moves up a level, corpses leave in the death phase.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] Unit test: eating transfers `yields`→`gain` and the prey is removed exactly once (in the death phase)
- [ ] Unit test: no double-eat — two eaters, one prey: the first in snapshot order receives `yields`, the second misses and stays hungry
- [ ] Unit test: an agent eaten earlier in the same act phase does not run its body-tick
- [ ] Unit test: eaten prey stays in the population (flagged) until phase 5, then is gone; the deaths counter reflects it
- [ ] Determinism: two same-seed runs produce identical eat outcomes and counter logs

### NADI-038 — `species/chara.yaml` + energy-per-level counter

**Description:**
The first consumer descriptor and the counter that makes the energy pyramid measurable. Closes the v4.1 DoD: Chara gain energy by eating Bindu; eaten Bindu die; the roughly 10% transfer ratio holds via `yields`/`gain`. (`species/chara.yaml`, counters.)

**What needs to be done:**
- Author `species/chara.yaml` per ARCHITECTURE §Species descriptive language: `ahara: Bindu`, `harvest` (gain, radius), `yields`, `needs`, `move`, `reproduce`, `life` — first-pass numbers targeting the ~10% energy transfer per level (the full brain and `seek_prey` land in v4.2; balance tuning in v4.3).
- Every Chara parameter lives in the YAML, none in engine code (ARCHITECTURE §Configuration and parameters).
- The **energy-per-trophic-level counter**: total (and mean) energy per species/level, emitted per tick as structured log / CSV alongside the existing counters (ARCHITECTURE §Counters and measurement).
- Renderers pick Chara up with a distinct glyph; the header shows the per-level counter (the additive snapshot schema means the web viewer needs no protocol change).
- A minimal hand-run check: Chara spawned onto a Bindu colony gain energy; eaten Bindu disappear.

**Dependencies:** NADI-037

**Expected result:**
Loading `chara.yaml` puts a second trophic level in the world with no engine edits; the counter shows energy flowing Bindu → Chara at roughly 10%.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] `species/chara.yaml` loads and validates; `ahara: Bindu` resolves through the generic path with no engine change
- [ ] The energy-per-level counter reports per-species/level totals each tick (structured log / CSV)
- [ ] Unit test: over a scripted eat, the level-1 → level-2 transfer ratio matches `yields`/`gain` (~10%)
- [ ] Eaten Bindu leave the population in the death phase; the per-species deaths counter reflects them
- [ ] Chara render with a distinct glyph; the header shows per-level energy
