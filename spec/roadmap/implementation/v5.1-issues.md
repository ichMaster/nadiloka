Issues for phase **v5.1 — Vyaghra and the third level** (version **v5 — Predators (Vyaghra)**), derived from the per-phase Tasks list in [ROADMAP.md](../../ROADMAP.md) (§v5.1). This file is scoped to a single phase; v5.2 and v5.3 have their own breakdowns. Each issue ships with the tests that encode its DoD (see [ARCHITECTURE.md](../../ARCHITECTURE.md) §Testing and CI).

v5.1 is the **abstraction-leak test of the whole design**: the third trophic level arrives as a **descriptor, not code**. `species/vyaghra.yaml` declares `ahara: Chara`, and the generic eat/lifecycle/brain path built in v4 must carry it **unchanged** — per ROADMAP §Notes on the ladder, "if a new species needs engine changes, the abstraction is leaking." Alongside it, the counters grow to three trophic levels (energy per level, the abundance pyramid) so the v5 readiness criterion becomes measurable before v5.2 makes the hunt dramatic.

## v5.1 — Vyaghra and the third level

### NADI-045 — `species/vyaghra.yaml`: the third trophic level

**Description:**
Author the Vyaghra species descriptor with `ahara: Chara` and prove that the third trophic level rides the **generic eat/lifecycle/brain path from v4 with zero engine changes** — the abstraction-leak test of the whole Jati design (ROADMAP §Notes on the ladder; ARCHITECTURE §Species descriptive language). (`species/vyaghra.yaml` + tests only.)

**What needs to be done:**
- Author `species/vyaghra.yaml` per ARCHITECTURE §Species descriptive language (Jati): `jati: Vyaghra`, `ahara: Chara`, plus `harvest`/`yields`/`needs`/`brain`/`reproduce`/`move`/`life` — a top predator: few, slow-breeding, energy-hungry, with the ~10% `gain`/`yields` transfer per level.
- The generic `ahara` resolution from NADI-036 wires Chara as prey with **no engine change**: eating transfers prey `yields` → predator `gain`, sets the prey's `eaten` flag, and removal happens in the death phase — first eater in snapshot order wins, no double-eat (ARCHITECTURE §Predation within a tick).
- Reuse the v4 lifecycle unchanged: reproduction (adulthood + energy threshold + cooldown + `MAX_POP` cap, paying the energy `cost`) and death (zero health, eaten, past `lifespan`) run entirely off the descriptor.
- Reuse the v4 brain unchanged: Wander/Hungry/Sleeping as the named pysm preset selected by the `brain` field; `move: seek_prey` targets the nearest Chara via the same path as NADI-040.
- Guard the claim in the diff itself: this issue touches `species/` and `tests/` only; any edit under the engine package means the abstraction is leaking and must be raised as a design problem, not patched in quietly.
- A renderer glyph for Vyaghra rides the existing per-species mapping (no new render path).

**Dependencies:** NADI-044, NADI-036

**Expected result:**
Dropping `vyaghra.yaml` into a running world adds a living, feeding, reproducing, dying third level — no engine edits.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] `species/vyaghra.yaml` loads and validates through the Jati loader (`ahara: Chara`; harvest/yields/needs/brain/reproduce/move/life all present)
- [ ] Vyaghra gain energy by eating Chara; the eaten Chara is flagged and removed in the death phase (first eater wins; no double-eat within a tick)
- [ ] Reproduction, death, and the brain run off the descriptor via the v4 path, unchanged
- [ ] Unit test: adding `vyaghra.yaml` wires a third level via `ahara` alone — the eat/lifecycle path reused from v4 works unchanged
- [ ] The change is descriptor-only: no modifications under the engine package

### NADI-046 — Three-level energy-pyramid counters

**Description:**
Grow the counters to three trophic levels: **energy per level** and the **abundance-pyramid** ordering check (Bindu > Chara > Vyaghra) — the measurable half of the v5 readiness criterion (ARCHITECTURE §Counters and measurement). Strictly observation: read-only over the world. (counters / observation layer.)

**What needs to be done:**
- Extend the energy-per-level counter from v4.1 to three levels: total energy held by Bindu, Chara, and Vyaghra per tick, with the trophic level derived from the `ahara` chain (tejas → Bindu → Chara → Vyaghra), not hardcoded species names.
- An abundance-pyramid counter: per-level population counts plus the ordering check `pop(Bindu) > pop(Chara) > pop(Vyaghra)`, emitted per tick as a boolean/violation counter the v5.3 guard can assert against.
- Per-level population and energy time-series in the structured counter log (log lines / CSV, ARCHITECTURE §Counters and measurement) consumable by tests and demos.
- The renderer header shows the three per-level populations (the live subset of the counters).
- Hold the read-only invariant: the new counters never mutate the world; observation on or off, a same-seed run is identical (ARCHITECTURE §Observation layer).

**Dependencies:** NADI-045

**Expected result:**
Every tick reports energy and population per trophic level; the pyramid ordering is a measurable, loggable fact ready for the v5.3 smoke guard.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] Energy-per-level counter covers all three levels, derived from the `ahara` chain (no hardcoded species names)
- [ ] Abundance-pyramid counter reports per-level populations and the Bindu > Chara > Vyaghra ordering check per tick
- [ ] Per-level time-series land in the structured counter log, consumable by the v5.3 smoke guard
- [ ] Unit test: a hand-built population yields the expected per-level energy totals and the correct pyramid ordering flag
- [ ] Read-only: counters never mutate the world; two same-seed runs produce byte-identical counter logs (determinism harness stays green)

## v5.1 scope notes

**Total effort:** ~2–4 days for a single developer (sequential: NADI-045 then NADI-046; the phase is deliberately small because the engine must not change).
