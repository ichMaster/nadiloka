## v1.3 — Reproduction and death

### NADI-023 — Reproduction

**Description:**
The population's engine: a Bindu that has reached adulthood, holds enough energy, is off cooldown, and is under `MAX_POP` spawns a newborn in a **nearby free cell** and pays the energy `cost`. The newborn is added **after the snapshot** — it acts from the next tick (ARCHITECTURE §Key invariants and pitfalls, snapshot iteration; the path NADI-019's pinning test guards). All gates and the cost come from the Jati `reproduce` block. (`nadiloka/digitant.py` body-tick + `nadiloka/world.py` spawn.)

**What needs to be done:**
- A reproduction check in the body-tick per the Jati `reproduce` block: adulthood (age past the threshold) + energy at or above the threshold + `repro_cd` expired + population under `MAX_POP`.
- Spawn placement: a nearby free cell chosen deterministically via the seeded World RNG (stable candidate ordering, ties broken by the RNG); no free cell in range means no spawn this tick.
- The parent pays the energy `cost` and its `repro_cd` resets; a parent that cannot pay does not spawn.
- The newborn is instantiated from the parent's Jati — genome placeholder equals the species defaults until v6 (ARCHITECTURE §Genome and heredity) — and added to the population **after the snapshot**, acting from the next tick.
- `MAX_POP` comes from the world config (ARCHITECTURE §Configuration and parameters); the cap is checked at spawn time and never exceeded.
- Counter: births-this-tick (feeds NADI-025's balance counters).

**Dependencies:** NADI-022

**Expected result:**
Rich, adult, rested Bindu spawn offspring next to themselves at a cost; the population grows where the light feeds it, deterministically, and never past the cap.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] Unit tests per gate: reproduction requires adulthood, the energy threshold, an expired cooldown, and population under `MAX_POP` — each gate blocks alone
- [ ] The newborn lands in a nearby free cell; no free cell in range means no spawn
- [ ] Unit test: the parent is debited the energy `cost` and its cooldown resets
- [ ] The newborn is added after the snapshot and does not act this tick
- [ ] Unit test: population never exceeds `MAX_POP` at the cap
- [ ] Determinism: same seed yields an identical birth sequence

### NADI-024 — Death and cleanup phase

**Description:**
Phase 5 of the tick (ARCHITECTURE §One tick cycle) does real work: remove digitants at zero health or past their `lifespan` — and **exactly those**. Removal is deferred to the cleanup phase so the act phase stays deterministic and nothing dies mid-iteration; this is the same mark-then-remove pattern predation reuses in v4 (§Predation within a tick). (`nadiloka/world.py`.)

**What needs to be done:**
- Implement the death/cleanup phase: sweep the population and remove agents whose health is zero (NADI-022's death-threshold state) or whose `age` exceeds the Jati `lifespan`.
- Removal happens only in phase 5 — earlier phases mark, never delete.
- A deterministic sweep order (id-sorted), so the removal log is reproducible run to run.
- Removed agents leave the population dict immediately and the spatial hash at the next rebuild-grid phase.
- Counter: deaths-this-tick, split by cause (starvation vs. old age) — feeds NADI-025's balance counters.

**Dependencies:** NADI-022

**Expected result:**
Starved and aged-out Bindu disappear from the world in phase 5 — no sooner, no later, and no bystanders.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] Unit test: over a mixed population (healthy, zero-health, past-lifespan), phase 5 removes exactly the qualifying agents
- [ ] No removal occurs outside the cleanup phase
- [ ] Removed agents are gone from the population and from the next tick's spatial hash
- [ ] deaths-this-tick counter, by cause, in the structured counter log
- [ ] Determinism: same seed yields an identical death sequence

### NADI-025 — `demos/bindu.py` + balance smoke run

**Description:**
The scenario that proves v1: `demos/bindu.py` runs a two-species-free world — Bindu alone on the moving Tejas light — with balance counters, and a fixed-seed K-tick smoke run encodes the **Layer 2 readiness criterion**: the population holds steadily, colonies bloom on lit zones and die as the light leaves, and `0 < pop < MAX_POP` throughout — no explosion, no extinction. (`demos/bindu.py` + `tests/`.)

**What needs to be done:**
- `demos/bindu.py`: World + Tejas + the Bindu population + the ASCII renderer over the demo loop (the `demos/light.py` shape from v0.3); one config per scenario — grid size, patch parameters, N, `MAX_POP`, tick count — with the seed always explicit (`--seed`, never implicit).
- Balance counters: births, deaths, and population per tick, joined with v1.2's mean energy/health — structured log lines the smoke test asserts against (ARCHITECTURE §Counters and measurement).
- Parameter tuning for a standing population, in the two homes only — the demo config and `species/bindu.yaml` (ARCHITECTURE §Configuration and parameters) — until the run holds.
- The smoke test: a fixed-seed run of K ticks asserts `0 < pop < MAX_POP` at every tick — the v1 readiness criterion as a permanent regression guard on the tuned parameters (§Testing and CI, smoke runs).
- Visual check: colonies visibly bloom on lit zones and thin out as patches fade and move (renderer + counters telling the same story).
- The determinism harness over the demo: two same-seed runs produce byte-identical counter logs.

**Dependencies:** NADI-023, NADI-024

**Expected result:**
Under reasonable parameters the Bindu population holds steadily on the moving light — colonies bloom and die with the patches — and the smoke test pins that behaviour against regression. v1 is done.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] `demos/bindu.py` runs Bindu on the moving light with the renderer and the balance counters
- [ ] Counters: births, deaths, population per tick (plus mean energy/health) as structured log lines
- [ ] Smoke test: a fixed-seed K-tick run keeps `0 < pop < MAX_POP` throughout — no explosion, no extinction
- [ ] Colonies bloom on lit zones and die back as the light leaves (visible in the renderer and the counters)
- [ ] Two same-seed runs produce byte-identical counter logs
- [ ] All tuning lives in the demo config and `bindu.yaml`, never in engine code
