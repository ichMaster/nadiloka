## v2.2 — Seek light

### NADI-028 — `seek_light` movement

**Description:**
Directed movement: `move: seek_light` picks the step maximizing local Tejas — gradient ascent over the neighbourhood — with ties among equally bright cells broken by the World's seeded RNG, so the walk stays deterministic under a fixed seed. Wired as the third `move` style beside `stay` and `wander` (ARCHITECTURE §Species descriptive language). (`nadiloka/digitant.py`, `nadiloka/jati.py`.)

**What needs to be done:**
- Extend the Jati `move` field validation with `seek_light` (`stay` | `wander` | `seek_light`); descriptors select it declaratively, no engine special-casing per species.
- Body-tick seek step: evaluate the Tejas intensity of the current cell and each **free, in-bounds** neighbour; step to the reachable cell with the maximum value, staying put when the current cell already wins — reusing the NADI-026 bounds/occupancy rules (no wrapping, never onto an occupied cell).
- Tie-break among equally bright reachable cells via the World's single seeded RNG, passed explicitly — deterministic under a fixed seed, no module-level `random.*` (ARCHITECTURE §RNG discipline).
- Seeking reads the field only — it never mutates Tejas; harvesting stays in the v1.2 feeding step, unchanged.
- Determinism: two same-seed runs with `move: seek_light` produce identical paths and byte-identical counter logs (the determinism harness, ARCHITECTURE §Testing and CI).

**Dependencies:** NADI-026

**Expected result:**
With `move: seek_light` Bindu climb the local light gradient toward patch centres, deterministically under a fixed seed.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] `move: seek_light` loads and validates in Jati alongside `stay` and `wander`
- [ ] Unit test: seek picks the brightest reachable (free, in-bounds) cell; stays put when the current cell is brightest
- [ ] Unit test: deterministic tie-break — equal-brightness neighbours resolve identically across same-seed runs
- [ ] Unit test: bounds and occupancy respected (the NADI-026 rules hold for seek steps)
- [ ] Seeking never mutates the Tejas field; two same-seed runs yield identical paths and counter logs

### NADI-029 — Three-regime demo and smoke

**Description:**
The payoff of v2: the style switch (`stay` / `wander` / `seek_light`) wired into the Bindu demo (NADI-025), counters that distinguish the regimes, and the smoke run encoding the v2 readiness criterion — three visibly different population regimes: stationary colonies, diffuse drift, and light-chasing clusters that deplete patches and form queues. (`demos/`, counters, `tests/`.)

**What needs to be done:**
- Wire the movement-style switch into the demo runner: a config/CLI option selecting `stay` / `wander` / `seek_light`, with the seed always explicit (`--seed`), per ARCHITECTURE §Configuration and parameters — nothing tunable hardcoded.
- Regime-distinguishing counters: **mean energy**, a **clustering** measure (dispersion of occupied cells / nearest-neighbour distance), and **field depletion** (Tejas drawn down under feeding clusters), emitted as structured log lines consumable by tests (ARCHITECTURE §Counters and measurement).
- Smoke test: each of the three styles runs K ticks at a fixed seed **without collapse** — population stays in a healthy band (`0 < pop < MAX_POP`), no explosion, no extinction.
- Smoke test, second half: the three styles produce **distinguishable counter profiles** (asserted orderings/thresholds — e.g. clustering: seek_light > stay > wander; displacement and depletion separate the regimes) — this assertion is the encoded Layer 3 readiness criterion.
- Determinism harness per style: two same-seed runs each produce byte-identical counter logs.
- A manual/visual pass of the demo per style: stationary colonies, diffuse drift, and light-chasing clusters with queues on patches are apparent in the renderer.

**Dependencies:** NADI-028, NADI-025

**Expected result:**
Switching the movement style yields three visibly and measurably different population regimes, each stable over the smoke run — v2 (Layer 3) is ready and v3 (Nadi) can build on it.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] The demo exposes the `stay` / `wander` / `seek_light` switch (config/CLI; explicit seed)
- [ ] Counters emitted per tick: mean energy, clustering, field depletion
- [ ] Smoke test: each style runs K ticks at a fixed seed with population in the healthy band (no collapse)
- [ ] Smoke test: the three styles produce distinguishable counter profiles (asserted, not eyeballed)
- [ ] Determinism: for each style, two same-seed runs produce byte-identical counter logs
- [ ] (Manual) the renderer shows the three regimes: colonies, drift, light-chasing clusters with queues
