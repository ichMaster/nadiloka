## v5.2 — Hunt and flee via Nadi

### NADI-047 — `hunt` and `flee` topics

**Description:**
The predator-prey topic pair over the v3 bus: a hunting Vyaghra broadcasts `hunt` in radius; a Chara receiving it (or directly sensing a predator) emits/reacts with `flee` — ARCHITECTURE §Topic protocol reserves exactly this pair for v5.2. Who emits and who listens is declared in the Jati `signals` field; delivery is deferred and deterministic per v3.1. (nadi topics + `species/*.yaml` `signals`.)

**What needs to be done:**
- Define the `hunt` message: emitted by a hunting (Hungry-mode) Vyaghra with `radius=r` addressing; the payload carries the sender's id and position (the flee direction in NADI-048 needs it); deferred delivery — emitted during the act phase, delivered at this tick's flush; reactions emitted during delivery go out the next tick (ARCHITECTURE §Event bus).
- Define the `flee` reaction: a Chara receiving `hunt` — or directly sensing a predator in range via the spatial hash — enters the fleeing condition and emits `flee`; the incoming message arrives on the brain as a pysm event with the payload in `cargo` (never `input=` — the pinned pitfall).
- Declare the wiring declaratively: `vyaghra.yaml` `signals` emits `hunt`; `chara.yaml` `signals` listens to `hunt` and emits `flee`; the subscription registry from NADI-032 honours the declaration — a species not listing a topic neither sends nor receives it.
- Radius delivery resolves recipients via the spatial grid in stable id-sorted order and hits **exactly** the in-range prey: no leaks beyond `r`, no misses inside it, FIFO across messages (ARCHITECTURE §Deterministic order).
- Per-topic counters (emitted/delivered) for `hunt` and `flee`, riding the v3.1 counter path.
- Bindu stay untouched: no `hunt`/`flee` in their `signals`, so the producer level never receives predator traffic.

**Dependencies:** NADI-045, NADI-032

**Expected result:**
A hunting Vyaghra's broadcast reaches exactly the in-range Chara, who react — all of it declared in YAML and delivered deterministically.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] `hunt` emits with radius addressing and a payload of sender id + position; deferred delivery at flush; replies ride the next tick
- [ ] `flee` fires on receiving `hunt` or on directly sensing a predator in range
- [ ] Unit test: `hunt` reaches exactly the in-range prey — radius boundary exact, recipients id-sorted, delivery order deterministic
- [ ] Unit test: signals honour the Jati `signals` declaration — a species without the topic listed neither emits nor receives it
- [ ] Payloads travel via `cargo` (pinning test — never `input=`); per-topic counters count hunts and flees

### NADI-048 — Movement responses: pursue and evade

**Description:**
Movement takes sides: hunting Vyaghra pursue via `seek_prey` (NADI-040), and fleeing Chara move **directionally away from the hunt's sender** — directed avoidance, not random scatter. This is the survival tool ROADMAP §Notes on the ladder promises the pyramid; direction choice stays deterministic, and hunts and flights become visible in the renderer and the counters. (digitant movement + brain modes.)

**What needs to be done:**
- The flee step: from the sender position in the `hunt` payload, pick the neighbour step maximizing distance from the sender — bounded grid, no wrap, occupancy respected; ties broken by the seeded world RNG (mirroring the v2.2 seek tie-break; ARCHITECTURE §RNG discipline).
- A fleeing condition on the Chara brain: entered on the flee trigger, decaying back to normal behaviour after a bounded number of ticks or once the threat is out of range; implemented with internal transitions so no `on_enter` re-fires (ARCHITECTURE §Key invariants and pitfalls).
- Priorities: fleeing overrides seeking/feeding on the prey side (survival beats hunger); a Hungry Vyaghra's `seek_prey` targets the nearest Chara as in NADI-040.
- Counters: hunts and flights per tick, alongside the per-topic message counters from NADI-047.
- Renderer: hunting Vyaghra and fleeing Chara visibly distinguishable (state-mapped glyphs on the existing per-species mapping); the header shows the hunt/flight counters.
- Keep the determinism harness green: two same-seed runs of a hunt scene produce byte-identical counter logs.

**Dependencies:** NADI-047, NADI-040

**Expected result:**
A hunting Vyaghra makes nearby Chara flee — the chase is visible in the renderer and measured in the counters (the v5.2 DoD).

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] Unit test: a fleeing Chara moves away from the hunt sender — the chosen step increases (or maximally preserves) distance to the sender, in-bounds, occupancy respected
- [ ] Direction choice is deterministic: same seed → same escape paths (ties via the world RNG)
- [ ] Fleeing overrides seek/feeding on the prey side and decays back after its bounded window (internal transitions; no `on_enter` re-fire)
- [ ] Hunts and flights counters increment; the renderer distinguishes hunting and fleeing states
- [ ] Smoke: a fixed-seed hunt scene runs K ticks without error, with byte-identical counter logs across two runs
