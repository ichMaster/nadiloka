Issues for phase **v2.1 — Random wandering** (version **v2 — Producer movement**), derived from the per-phase Tasks list in [ROADMAP.md](../../ROADMAP.md) (§v2.1). This file is scoped to a single phase; v2.2 gets its own breakdown. Each issue ships with the tests that encode its DoD (see [ARCHITECTURE.md](../../ARCHITECTURE.md) §Testing and CI).

v2.1 gives Bindu their first legs: a **`move`** field in the Jati descriptor (`stay` | `wander`) and a body-tick wander step — one seeded random step to a free neighbour cell, bounded, no wrapping — while `move: stay` keeps v1 behaviour **byte-identical**. The observation layer grows alongside: the renderer shows the motion and a mean-displacement counter measures it. Every random draw comes from the World's single seeded RNG (ARCHITECTURE §RNG discipline), so same-seed runs replay identical paths.

## v2.1 — Random wandering

### NADI-026 — The `move` field + wander step

**Description:**
The first movement in Nadiloka: a **`move`** style field in the Jati descriptor (`stay` | `wander`) and a body-tick wander step — a seeded random step to a free neighbour cell, in bounds, no edge wrapping — while `move: stay` is a strict no-op that keeps v1 runs byte-identical. (`nadiloka/jati.py`, `nadiloka/digitant.py`, `species/bindu.yaml`.)

**What needs to be done:**
- Extend the Jati loader (NADI-018) with a validated `move` field (`stay` | `wander`), per ARCHITECTURE §Species descriptive language; default `stay`, so `species/bindu.yaml` and any existing descriptor load unchanged.
- Body-tick movement in the act phase: with `move: wander`, take one seeded random step to a **free** neighbour cell — in bounds, **no wrapping** (ARCHITECTURE §World model), never onto an occupied cell (occupancy via the NADI-019 spatial hash); if no free neighbour exists, stay put this tick.
- All randomness through the World's single `random.Random`, passed explicitly — no module-level `random.*` calls (ARCHITECTURE §RNG discipline).
- `move: stay` is a **strict no-op**: the movement path is never entered, and a fixed-seed v1-style run produces byte-identical counter logs before and after this change.
- The spatial hash reflects new positions via the existing rebuild-grid phase (ARCHITECTURE §One tick cycle) — no extra index maintenance; verify occupancy checks within the act phase stay consistent under snapshot iteration.
- Determinism: two runs with the same seed and config produce identical movement paths (the determinism harness, ARCHITECTURE §Testing and CI).

**Dependencies:** NADI-019, NADI-018

**Expected result:**
With `move: wander` Bindu drift over the grid, never leaving bounds or stacking on an occupied cell; with `move: stay` v1 behaviour replays byte-identically.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] `move` field (`stay` | `wander`) loads and validates in Jati; `stay` is the default
- [ ] Unit test: a wander step stays in bounds (no wrapping) and respects occupancy (never lands on an occupied cell)
- [ ] Unit test: `move: stay` is a strict no-op — a fixed-seed run produces counter logs byte-identical to v1
- [ ] Unit test: determinism — two same-seed runs produce identical paths (positions per tick)
- [ ] All movement randomness drawn from the World's seeded RNG (no module-level `random.*`)

### NADI-027 — Motion observability

**Description:**
The observation layer for movement: the v0.3 ASCII renderer (NADI-009) shows moving digitants and a **mean-displacement** counter makes the difference measurable — `wander` and `stay` become visibly and numerically distinct, the seeing-and-measuring half of the v2.1 DoD. Strictly read-only over the world (ARCHITECTURE §Observation layer). (`nadiloka/render.py`, counters.)

**What needs to be done:**
- Renderer: digitant glyphs render at their current positions each tick, so wandering Bindu visibly drift across the grid frame to frame.
- A mean-displacement counter (mean distance of live digitants from their spawn positions), emitted as a structured counter-log line and shown in the renderer header (ARCHITECTURE §Counters and measurement).
- Under `move: stay` the counter reads zero for all ticks; under `move: wander` it grows — wander vs stay measurably different, not just visibly.
- Read-only invariant: with the renderer/counter attached or absent, a same-seed run produces identical counter logs — observation never mutates the world (ARCHITECTURE §Key invariants and pitfalls).
- A short manual check of the Bindu demo with `move: wander`: motion is apparent in the terminal at reasonable parameters.

**Dependencies:** NADI-026, NADI-009

**Expected result:**
Watching the demo, wandering Bindu visibly drift over the grid; the mean-displacement counter cleanly separates `wander` from `stay`.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] Renderer shows digitants at their updated positions each tick (motion is visible in the demo)
- [ ] Mean-displacement counter emitted in the structured counter log and shown in the renderer header
- [ ] Unit test: mean displacement is zero under `stay` and positive under `wander` over a fixed-seed run
- [ ] Read-only: renderer on or off, a same-seed run produces identical counter logs
